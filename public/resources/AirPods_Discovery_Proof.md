# Phase 1: Discovery & Problem Hypothesis

**Project:** AirPods CustomFit Pro (eCornell Product Management)
**Core Competency:** Choosing the Right Problem & Defining Value.

## The Problem Space

Generic earbud fit is not just a comfort issue—it is an acoustic and stability failure that impacts high-value "Enterprise Commuters" who rely on AirPods for 8+ hours of continuous professional use.

## Proof of Iteration

### Iteration 1: The Personal Nuisance (Pre-Certification)

* **Hypothesis:** "People find AirPods uncomfortable."
* **Pivot Reason:** After applying the **Cornell Tech ctech101** framework, I realized "comfort" is too subjective and doesn't justify a premium hardware intervention.

### Iteration 2: The Acoustic Seal Failure (Course Guided)

* **Research:** In-ear testing revealed that sub-optimal fit leads to a 30% drop in passive noise cancellation efficiency.
* **Refined Hypothesis:** "Professional users sacrifice audio fidelity and call clarity because generic industrial design fails to create a consistent acoustic seal across diverse ear anatomies."
* **Success of Problem Selection:** This problem anchors into **Pain Level**, **Frequency (Daily)**, and **Differentiation** (Universal fit vs. Anatomical fit).

## Artifact Summary

* **Persona:** The High-Value Commuter (Requires zero-re-adjustment during multi-tasking).
* **Value Prop:** Studio-grade isolation through a standard consumer device.
