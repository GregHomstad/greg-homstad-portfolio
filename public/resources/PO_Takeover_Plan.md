# 30-Day Product Owner Takeover Plan

**Strategic Leadership & Role Transition Framework**

## Executive Summary

This framework outlines the strategic shift from a technical Individual Contributor (Analyst) role to a high-impact Product Owner (PO). It focuses on backlog ownership, stakeholder alignment, and establishing a clear boundary between technical execution and product strategy.

---

## Phase 1: Alignment & Audit (Week 1)

**Goal:** Establish clear role boundaries and audit current roadmap health.

- **Role Clarification:** Align with PM and Project Managers to confirm the PM (Vision/Strategy) vs. PO (Backlog/Execution) relationship.
- **Backlog Audit:** Perform a deep-dive into the current backlog to identify the next two sprints of high-priority work.
- **Touchpoint Integration:** Establish recurring Weekly Backlog Grooming (Dev Team) and Bi-Weekly PO/PM Strategy Syncs.

## Phase 2: Backlog Ownership (Week 2)

**Goal:** Transition technical specs into actionable user-centric stories.

- **Epic Decomposition:** Break down complex enterprise epics into INVEST-compliant user stories.
- **Acceptance Criteria:** Define rigorous "Definition of Done" for all upcoming features.
- **Feedback Loop:** Partner with PMs to ensure story mapping reflects the long-term product vision.

## Phase 3: Sprint Leadership (Week 3)

**Goal:** Build dev team confidence and lead planning ceremonies.

- **Sprint Prep:** Lead the prioritization efforts for upcoming sprints, ensuring all dependencies are identified.
- **Communication Lead:** Act as the primary point of contact for developer clarification, reducing friction between business and engineering.
- **Visual Mapping:** Draft simple roadmap visualizations (2-sprint horizon) to communicate progress to stakeholders.

## Phase 4: Full PO Control (Week 4)

**Goal:** Establish the permanent handoff process and measure success.

- **Ceremony Mastery:** Fully lead Sprint Planning and Backlog Refinement ceremonies.
- **Process Documentation:** Formalize the handoff: *PM Epics → PO User Stories → Dev Refinement.*
- **Success Metrics:** Measured by:
  - Zero ambiguity during sprint planning.
  - Backlog always "2 Sprints Ready."
  - PM liberated to focus on 100% strategic initiatives.
